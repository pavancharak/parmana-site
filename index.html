The Real Problem
The problem is not that AI makes decisions.

The real problem begins when AI becomes the source of the authority that governs people, organizations, and critical systems.
We believe
AI should be intelligent.
AI analyzes, reasons, learns, and executes with speed and capability that no human process can match. This is its purpose.
We also believe
Authority must remain human.
Humans establish the authority, define the boundaries, and determine the policies within which AI operates. This responsibility cannot be delegated.
I
Separate responsibilities
Intelligence and authority are distinct functions. Conflating them is where trust breaks down.
II
The boundary must be clear
Parmana ensures the line between AI capability and human authority is cryptographically enforced not assumed.
III
Speed without compromise
When responsibilities remain separate, AI delivers speed and intelligence without ever displacing the humans who govern it.
